(a) Describe the data and each variable it contains. Tidy the data, preparing it for a data analysis.
Solution:

The data has 50 rows for 50 US states & 12 columns.

state.abb: character vector of 2-letter abbreviations for the state names.

state.area: numeric vector of state areas (in square miles).

state.center: list with components named x and y giving the approximate geographic center of each state in negative longitude and latitude. Alaska and Hawaii are placed just off the West Coast.

state.division: factor giving state divisions (New England, Middle Atlantic, South Atlantic, East South Central, West South Central, East North Central, West North Central, Mountain, and Pacific).

state.name: character vector giving the full state names.

state.region: factor giving the region (Northeast, South, North Central, West) that each state belongs to.

Population: population estimate as of July 1, 1975

Income: per capita income (1974)

Illiteracy: illiteracy (1970, percent of population)

Life Exp: life expectancy in years (196971)

Murder: murder and non-negligent manslaughter rate per 100,000 population (1976)

HS Grad: percent high-school graduates (1970)

Frost: mean number of days with minimum temperature below freezing (19311960) in capital or large city

Area: land area in square miles

References: US state Facts & Figure - R documentation retrieved from https://stat.ethz.ch/R-manual/R-devel/library/datasets/html/state.html

Solution:

I believe there is a fundamental problem with trying to find a relationship between the crimes data and education data because of following reasons:
1. Crimes data captures crimes over several decades from around 1975 to around 2018. Whereas education data is a census captured in a single year.
2. Moreover, it is not stated that which year the education data was captured, like 2000 or 2010. So it is not possible to filter down the crimes data to the particular year to which the education data corresponds.
3. Therefore I think the given data is not enough to establish a reliable relationship between education and crimes. As a mitigation, we can try to guess the census year by comparing the total population of the city (obtained by adding the the Total column) to external sources of census data and then use that year to filter the crime data.
